General Tips:

  1. On Offense:
    • First three kickers will be fastest runners and kick short bunts to get on base.
    • When bases are loaded, boot the ball. If on base, wait until the ball is caught or touches the ground, then sprint to score.
  2. Track the Ball Early:
    • Watch the opponent's planting foot to determine the direction of the ball.
    • As soon as the ball is kicked, locate it in the air.
    • Keep your eyes on the ball at all times and judge its trajectory to determine where it will land.
  3. Get in Position:
    • Move your feet quickly to get directly underneath the ball. Don't try to reach for it; instead, position your body where the ball is likely to come down.
    • Keep your knees slightly bent and your weight balanced on the balls of your feet for stability and quick adjustments.
  4. Set Your Hands:
    • For a high pop-up: Position your hands in a diamond shape (thumbs and index fingers touching) if the ball is coming straight down or at a slight angle. This gives you a wide, secure surface to catch the ball.
    • For a lower or shallow-angle ball: Use the "basket catch" technique by holding your hands together with palms up and fingers pointing out, like forming a basket.
  5. Body Positioning:
    • Keep your body square to the ball, with your chest facing where the ball is coming from.
    • Lean slightly forward to help absorb the ball into your hands and prevent it from bouncing out.
  6. Watch the Ball Into Your Hands:
    • Don't take your eyes off the ball until it’s securely in your hands. Many drops happen because players look away too early, anticipating their next move.
  7. Secure the Catch:
    • Once the ball hits your hands, grip it firmly with both hands to ensure it doesn’t bounce out.
    • If you feel unsteady after the catch, bring the ball into your chest to secure it.
  8. Practice Situational Awareness:
    • Communicate clearly by calling “I got it!” if you’re confident in making the catch.
